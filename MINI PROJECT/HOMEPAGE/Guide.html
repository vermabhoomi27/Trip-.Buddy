DELHI

Red Fort

The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, is a historic fort in Delhi, India, that historically served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors. Emperor Shah Jahan commissioned the construction of the Red Fort on 12 May 1639, when he decided to shift his capital from Agra to Delhi.

Lotus Temple

The Lotus Temple is a Baháʼí House of Worship located in New Delhi, India. It was completed in December 1986. Notable for its lotus-like shape, it has become a prominent attraction in the city. Like all Bahá’í Houses of Worship, the Lotus Temple is open to all, regardless of religion or any other qualification.

India Gate

The India Gate (formerly known as All India War Memorial) is a war memorial located near the Rajpath (officially called Kartavya Path) on the eastern edge of the "ceremonial axis" of New Delhi. It stands as a memorial to 74,187 soldiers of the Indian Army who died between 1914 and 1921 in the First World War.

Qutub Minar

The Qutb Minar, also spelled Qutub Minar and Qutab Minar, is a minaret and "victory tower" that forms part of the Qutb complex, which lies at the site of Delhi's oldest fortified city, Lal Kot, founded by the Tomar Rajputs. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the Mehrauli area of South Delhi, India.

RAJASTHAN

Hawa Mahal

The Hawa Mahal, also known as the "Palace of Winds," is located in Jaipur, Rajasthan. It is a five-story palace made of pink sandstone, built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh to allow royal women to observe street festivals without being seen.

Amer Fort

Amer Fort is a magnificent fort located in Amer, Rajasthan, near Jaipur. Built in 1592 by Raja Man Singh, the fort is known for its artistic Hindu-style architecture, with large ramparts, cobbled paths, and beautiful views of Maota Lake.

Udaipur City Palace

The Udaipur City Palace is located on the eastern bank of Lake Pichola in Udaipur, Rajasthan. Built over 400 years starting in 1559, this palace complex is famous for its beautiful architecture, sprawling gardens, and stunning views of the lake.

Jaisalmer Fort

Jaisalmer Fort, also known as "Sonar Quila" or the "Golden Fort," is located in Jaisalmer, Rajasthan. Built in 1156 AD by the Rajput ruler Rawal Jaisal, the fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is one of the largest forts in the world.

MAHARASHTRA

Gateway of India

The Gateway of India is an iconic monument located in Mumbai, Maharashtra. Built in 1924, it was erected to commemorate the arrival of King George V and Queen Mary to India. It is a popular tourist spot and a symbol of Mumbai's heritage.

Ajanta Caves

The Ajanta Caves, located in Aurangabad, Maharashtra, are a series of 29 rock-cut caves dating back to the 2nd century BCE. These caves are renowned for their stunning Buddhist paintings and sculptures and are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Elephanta Caves

The Elephanta Caves, located on Elephanta Island near Mumbai, are a collection of ancient rock-cut temples. Dating back to the 5th to 8th centuries, these caves are dedicated to Lord Shiva and are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Shaniwar Wada

Shaniwar Wada is a historic fortification in Pune, Maharashtra. Built in 1732 by the Peshwas of the Maratha Empire, it is known for its rich history and architecture, with intricate carvings and sprawling gardens.

TAMIL NADU

Meenakshi Temple

The Meenakshi Temple, located in Madurai, Tamil Nadu, is one of the most famous Hindu temples in India. Dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi and Lord Sundareswarar, it is known for its stunning architecture and colorful sculptures.

Mahabalipuram

Mahabalipuram, also known as Mamallapuram, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located on the Coromandel Coast. It is famous for its ancient rock-cut temples, monuments, and the Shore Temple.

Brihadeeswarar Temple

The Brihadeeswarar Temple, located in Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu, is one of the largest temples in India. Built by Raja Raja Chola I in the 11th century, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and an example of Dravidian architecture.

Ooty

Ooty, officially known as Udhagamandalam, is a hill station in Tamil Nadu. It is famous for its scenic landscapes, tea gardens, and pleasant weather, making it a popular summer destination.

UTTARAKHAND

Kedarnath Temple

Kedarnath Temple, located in the Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand, is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites and one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of India.

Nainital Lake

Nainital Lake, located in the hill station of Nainital, Uttarakhand, is a famous natural lake shaped like a crescent. It is surrounded by picturesque hills and is a major tourist attraction.

Haridwar

Haridwar is a sacred city in Uttarakhand, known for its ghats along the Ganges River. It is a major Hindu pilgrimage site, where the famous Ganga Aarti takes place every evening.

Valley of Flowers

The Valley of Flowers is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Uttarakhand. It is known for its vibrant alpine flowers and stunning landscapes, attracting nature lovers and trekkers.

JAMMU & KASHMIR

Dal Lake

Dal Lake, located in Srinagar, Jammu & Kashmir, is famous for its houseboats and shikaras. The lake is a major tourist attraction and is surrounded by gardens and the majestic Himalayan mountains.

Gulmarg

Gulmarg, located in Jammu & Kashmir, is known for its skiing resorts and the Gulmarg Gondola, one of the highest cable cars in the world. It is a popular hill station and skiing destination.

Vaishno Devi Temple

The Vaishno Devi Temple, located in Katra, Jammu & Kashmir, is one of the most visited pilgrimage sites in India. The temple is dedicated to Goddess Vaishno Devi and attracts millions of devotees annually.

Pahalgam

Pahalgam, located in Jammu & Kashmir, is known for its beautiful landscapes, trekking trails, and lush green valleys. It is a popular tourist destination and the starting point for the Amarnath Yatra pilgrimage.

PUNJAB

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is located in Amritsar, Punjab. It is the holiest site for Sikhs and is famous for its stunning architecture and the community kitchen that serves free meals to thousands daily.

Wagah Border

Wagah Border, located near Amritsar, is a ceremonial border point between India and Pakistan. The daily flag-lowering ceremony is a major attraction, showcasing patriotism and unity.

Jallianwala Bagh

Jallianwala Bagh, situated in Amritsar, is a historic garden memorializing the tragic massacre of 1919. It stands as a reminder of India's struggle for independence.

Qila Mubarak

Qila Mubarak is a historic fortress in Patiala, Punjab. Built in the 18th century, it showcases a blend of Sikh and Mughal architecture and houses several museums and artifacts.

ASSAM

Kamakhya Temple

Kamakhya Temple, located in Guwahati, Assam, is one of the oldest and most revered Shakti temples in India. It is dedicated to Goddess Kamakhya and is a significant spiritual center.

Kaziranga National Park

Kaziranga National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Assam. It is renowned for its one-horned rhinoceroses, lush green landscapes, and diverse wildlife.

Majuli Island

Majuli, the world's largest river island, is located in the Brahmaputra River in Assam. Known for its vibrant culture, scenic beauty, and ancient monasteries, it is a cultural hub of Assam.

Sivasagar

Sivasagar is a historic town in Assam, known for its Ahom-era monuments, including Rang Ghar, Talatal Ghar, and Sivasagar Lake, showcasing Assam's rich history.

MIZORAM

Phawngpui Peak

Phawngpui Peak, also known as the Blue Mountain, is the highest peak in Mizoram. It offers breathtaking views and is a haven for trekkers and nature enthusiasts.

Vangchhia

Vangchhia is an archaeological site in Mizoram, featuring ancient megalithic structures and stone carvings, offering insights into the region's history and culture.

Reiek Tlang

Reiek Tlang is a serene mountain near Aizawl, offering panoramic views of the surrounding valleys. It is a popular spot for hiking and picnics.

Serchhip

Serchhip is a picturesque town in Mizoram, home to Vantawng Falls, Mizoram's highest waterfall, and lush green landscapes that captivate visitors.

WEST BENGAL

Howrah Bridge

Howrah Bridge, an engineering marvel, is a cantilever bridge over the Hooghly River in Kolkata, West Bengal. It is an iconic symbol of the city and a busy landmark.

Darjeeling

Darjeeling, a charming hill station in West Bengal, is famous for its tea plantations, stunning views of the Himalayas, and the UNESCO-listed Darjeeling Himalayan Railway.

Sundarbans

The Sundarbans, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the world's largest mangrove forest and is home to the Bengal tiger. It is a unique ecosystem that attracts nature lovers.

Victoria Memorial

Victoria Memorial is a stunning white marble building in Kolkata, dedicated to Queen Victoria. It houses a museum and is surrounded by lush gardens.

HARYANA

Kurukshetra

Kurukshetra, a historic town in Haryana, is known as the land of the Mahabharata. It houses several temples, museums, and the Brahma Sarovar, a sacred water tank.

Sultanpur National Park

Sultanpur National Park, located near Gurugram, is a birdwatcher's paradise, with over 250 species of migratory and resident birds.

Pinjore Gardens

Pinjore Gardens, also known as Yadavindra Gardens, is a 17th-century Mughal garden near Panchkula, offering a beautiful blend of history and nature.

Morni Hills

Morni Hills, located near Chandigarh, is a serene hill station in Haryana. It is known for its scenic views, trekking trails, and adventure activities.

GOA

Baga Beach

Baga Beach is one of the most popular beaches in Goa, known for its lively nightlife, water sports, and beach shacks offering delicious seafood and drinks.

Basilica of Bom Jesus

The Basilica of Bom Jesus, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a 400-year-old church in Old Goa. It is renowned for housing the relics of St. Francis Xavier.

Dudhsagar Falls

Dudhsagar Falls, located on the Mandovi River, is one of the tallest waterfalls in India. It offers stunning views and is surrounded by lush green forests.

Fort Aguada

Fort Aguada is a 17th-century Portuguese fort in North Goa. It offers panoramic views of the Arabian Sea and is a popular spot for history enthusiasts and photographers.

Andhra Pradesh

Tirupati

Tirupati is a city in the Chittoor district of Andhra Pradesh, famous for the Tirumala Venkateswara Temple, one of the holiest Hindu pilgrimage sites.

Amaravati Stupa

The Amaravati Stupa is a heritage Buddhist site located in Amaravathi, showcasing ancient Buddhist art and architecture.

Kailasagiri

Kailasagiri is a hilltop park in Visakhapatnam with panoramic views of the sea, lush greenery, and iconic statues of Shiva and Parvati.

Borra Caves

The Borra Caves are natural limestone caves located in the Ananthagiri hills, known for their stalactites and stalagmites.

Arunachal Pradesh

Tawang Monastery

Tawang Monastery is the largest monastery in India, located in Tawang, and an important center for Mahayana Buddhism.

Bhalukpong

Bhalukpong is a serene town known for its picturesque views, river rafting, and as a gateway to Pakhui Wildlife Sanctuary.

Sela Pass

The Sela Pass is a high-altitude mountain pass known for its scenic beauty and strategic importance in Arunachal Pradesh.

Ziro Valley

Ziro Valley is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its stunning landscapes, rice fields, and the vibrant Apatani tribal culture.
